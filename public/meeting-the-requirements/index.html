Getting Through AM

Looking at the Handbook.January.2020.pdf today, and trying to make sure that I've met/had waived all of the requirements that I need to at this point. There's a ton of different requirements, so that's why I'm noting my progress through them all!

Coursework

The handbook states that the in order to complete the requirements for a PhD in applied math, you must have 48 units of coursework outside of the dissertation, and 18 units of dissertation. Those units must be spread across multiple areas:

  • 24 units of core courses
    • 527 A/B (6)
    • 575 A/B (6)
    • 583 A/B (6)
    • 586 A/B/C (6)
  • 9 units of 500 level math courses
  • 9 units of courses outside of math
  • 9 elective units

Qualification

The quals process for AM is pretty straightforward. You just need to pass with reasonable scores all three final exams from the core courses. I'm well on my way for that, since I've satisfied Stepanov for fall, and the other two were waived. Auditing courses for the second half this semester, we'll see how that goes.

Comprehensive Exam

Basically, you choose a paper? And then write about it? And then get grilled on it? More or less.

  • Written report on the research paper and background material (basically, same exact thing as the presentation).
  • 'Professional Resume' and website. I'll just need to update things a bit now.
  • A road map for the rest of my studies.
    • This includes submitting the plan of study and oral exam comittee selection forms on GradPath. My Plan of Study doc will have to be updated a bit, and removed from AME.
  • Sitting for the actual 'oral examination.'

Dissertation

The big kahuna. This is pretty straightforward as well. Basically, a significant work of original research in my sub-field of choosing, that is 'of sufficient quality to be published in a reputable journal.'

Normal committee administrivia follow, and you have to have a copy of it out to the outside reviewer, the committee, and the grad college three weeks before the final defense date.

Research Proposal

This is an almost ancillary requirement that's sort of tacked on to the end here, but we have to submit at somepoint during the program a fully written research proposal in the style of an NIH or NSF post-doctoral fellowship proposal. I can probably knock this out sometime in the near future, and not have to worry about it anymore. I should ask around to see what the process for doing so is like.
