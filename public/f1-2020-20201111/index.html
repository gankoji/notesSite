Sakhir

Started out the evening in Bahrain, working on some time trial laps to try and find my pace again. Almost completely accidentally, I had a bit of an epiphany as I was coming out of the last corner to start my first hot lap: I overcooked it into the turn, and I held on the brakes, trail braking to the apex, and actually made the most beautiful exit out of that corner that I'd ever made!

This single incident made me realize how little of the training I just spent a month on I've actually been applying. I haven't really been practicing my fundamentals, or trying to bring them in to the overall race pace practice I've been doing. So, of course, I set out to then replicate this experiment across the whole lap, starting with just turn 1. After that realization, though, it was only 2 or 3 times through the turns 1-2-3 complex that I was beating my old ghost through there. Turn 4 came quite easily, and even turn 8 (and 10!) is quite easy for me to navigate now near the limit, as I use trail braking to adjust not only my speed when necessary, but the pitch and grip of the car as I corner. While I was suffering a bit from putting all of the corners together perfectly, after about 10 laps at Sakhir I was back to within a half second of my all time record there, this time without any assists whatsoever.

Then, the GP

This was just for fun. I setup a single session practice/one shot qualy/5 lap race GP weekend with the AI at 90 in Bahrain, and went out and smoked 'em. 1:17.4 was my hotlap, which I was a bit disappointed with after nailing a 1:17.84 on qualy sim in practice, but it was enough for pole by 4 tenths. The GP was a bit of a rocky start, as usual, since I got dive-bombed by bottas in turn 1, but I got him back on the back straight and never looked back.

Australian GP

Kicked off season 6 of 'my team' with a massively disappointing second place this evening, and I found there were enough problems to sit down and write about it. I really feel I should start doing this sort of learning/training journal type thing more often, but we'll see how I get on with it.

Practice

FP1+2 went my way the whole time, really, this grand prix weekend. From track acclimitisation lap all the way through race pace and qualy sim, I was on top. After having spent all that time focusing on trail braking and pushing apex speed at Bahrain earlier, I felt I was really on my way to some shit hot laps in Australia. Qualy sim lap best was a 1:18.0 ish.

Qualifying

Three laps total. The first was 1.17.88, which my teammate beat by 0.090. Went out for a second run on fresh rubber and absolutely smashed that by 8 tenths, all the way to a 1:17.006. He didn't recover. Went out for a third time right at the end of the session and put in a hotlap that was at least 4 tenths faster than the previous, but for some reason the game threw it out. I'm still taking credit for a 1:16.58, which is an absolutely ridiculous lap in Australia. Final delta (not including the actual fastest lap) between me and 2nd place (Albon) was 6 tenths. 6 fucking tenths.

Race

Thiiiiiis is where it all fell apart. Lights out and I'm on the back foot, losing out to Albon and Vettel by turn 1, and having to claw my way back into the fight. I don't get Vettel until turn 12, the end of the back straight, and by then I'm legit 2 seconds down to my teammate. I realize by lap 4 that I'm not accounting for the lack of DRS in my braking points at the ends of the straights, and I try to adjust for that the final lap, but by then I'm 5 seconds down, overdriving, hating myself, and just not having a good time.

The thing I didn't realize until I'd calmed down a bit, and processed the race, was that Albon wasn't really that fast in the race. His best lap (we traded purples a few times) was a 1:18.354, which was about 5 tenths off his qualy hotlap. My best time was a 1:18.75, with was more than 2 seconds off my qualy time. This leads to a couple of things, one of which is the big question:

Do my tires having two laps on them matter here?

I used all three sets of softs in qualy. I have to start on one of those in the race. I thought that you went onto the tires you qualified on if you were in the top 10, but maybe the other guys didn't have to do that and they got fresh fresh rubber for the start of the race?

The other thing I noticed is that I did an absolutely shit job of remembering my fundamentals. I got to wheel to wheel racing and lost my head a bit, especially being down so many spots by the first corner. I quit trail braking, I quit pushing deeper into the corners and pushing apex speed, and overall just sort of fell flat. I fell deep back into my old habits which got me here in the first place. This is somewhat to be expected, I suppose, given how long I drove with them. It's going to be a long road towards 'recovery' and embedding those new habits, but certainly 2 hours a week for 4 weeks in that master class isn't enough to just throw up my hands and say 'I'm cured!' Practice.
