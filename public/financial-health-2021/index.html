The State of the Union

Income

Current base salary is $138,000/yr, paid twice monthly. My forecasting figures also include the 15% SCA bonus, paid quarterly, as well as an estimated 10% performance bonus come mid-year review time (around August). That puts us at $172,500 total cash income (gross) this year.

There is also a possibility of a stock bonus at review time, although I'm not counting on that in my forecast. One thing I don't have modeled in the script is the vesting of the stock bonus that was included in my sign-on package with MS ($80,000, vested over 4 years, now valued at $85,000 and change). $26,000 ish of that will vest this year on 7/15.

Total income in 2020 was $153,380, which included large influence from the $46,162.83 paycheck I received the first pay period from MS, which included all of the cash for my sign-on bonus and relocation expenses.

While I have a very optimistic budget set of $6,500/month, we blow well past that quite often. December's expenses were over $10,000! End of year, holidays, and birthdays tend to do that, but such is life.

Looking Ahead and Changes

We are now at 15.52% of our target of $2,500,000 for FI. This is a major improvement over this time last year, of more than 3 percentage points. With a few changes to my witholdings on my paycheck (increasing 401k to 12%, to hit yearly max of $19,500 and get the full MS match, and upping ESPP purchases to 5% of pay), we're looking at a savings goal for this year of $46,033, and a savings rate of 29.87% of post-tax income. This puts us on track to reach the FI target in just over 14 years, where I'll be 47, and Penny will be just on her way out the door to adult life.

Adding the Roth contributions from here forward, we can improve that even further to a total save of $52,033, 33.77%, and 13.6 years to retirement.
