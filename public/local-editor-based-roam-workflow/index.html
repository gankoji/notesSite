App Idea - Local editor based roam ish workflow for noting

I wrote this in Evernote as well, but I figured I'd give OneNote a shot since I'll likely soon have a subscription to o365 and then it will make sense to keep everything in OneDrive, app integrations compound, etc.

Anyway, on to it. I had the idea earlier tonight for a good starter app to whet my chops a bit when it comes to javascript development. I'm a bit disappointed with the available apps out there for note taking. Although they've improved significantly over the last few years, especially in the Zettelkasten space (Zettlr, Obsidian) and there's lots of wiki based solutions (vim wiki, markdown editors, vs code and atom plugins, etc), there's also been a real game changer in Roam Research.

Roam is important for a lot of reasons. It's built with Clojure, which is really starting to make it's way into the forefront of modern development. It's a webapp, which means no installation and no fuss, just run it straight from your browser. [This comes with downsides, as well, which I'll get to]. Perhaps most importantly, though, is the fact that it implements, in an incredibly simple fashion, one of the most important pieces of Ted Nelson's Xanadu concept for the web: bidirectional links and graph connections. Transclusion (the compiling of documents from pieces of others, like putting together an article from a set of notes on a topic without much or any manual editing intervention) is a natural byproduct of this.

Put all of the nice features together and Roam is really a powerhouse right now in the note taking app scene, and for good reason. It's easy to use, easy to understand, and has incredible power.

However, Roam has its issues as well. First and foremost is the fact that actual writing experience is dreadful. It's still a mostly mouse and arrow key driven experience, which is hard to sell to an old emacs/spacemacs diehard like me. I'm just too committed to keeping my hands on the keyboard at this point in my life. Second, and probably more importantly, is the fact that all of the notes you place on Roam are trivially accessible by anyone, from anywhere, at anytime. There is no concept of privacy in this app. This is problematic for many reasons, but probably the most practical one at the moment for me is that I can't use it for work notes.

So, I've decided to toss my hat in the ring and try to drum up a note taking app of my own. Chances are that it won't be much good, but I figure that a) it will be a good opportunity for me to learn more about the modern desktop development frameworks that are out there, and b) by using more modern tools than those that I've been used to [i.e. eLisp, or god forbid C], I probably stand half a chance of getting something working before my attention on the topic wears off.
