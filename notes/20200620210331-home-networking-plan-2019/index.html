Home Networking Plan Friday, December 20, 2019 4:36 PM

So, I have some notes on this on paper somewhere at the house, but I've decided to get them into Evernote while I'm sitting around doing a whole lotta nothing.

The goal: take advantage of old enterprise hardware I have on hand to create a more usable, functional network for the home. I have some rather grandiose plans, and they will probably take quite a while to get spun up, but that's ok by me.

I want to setup a NAS on the network to support a) local backup, b) media sharing, and c) other general network/serving tasks (maybe a print server?). I think that PLEX is a good software solution for serving media from a central server, but I don't know much about it. I also don't have a whole lot of media (electronically stored) atm. I'll need to think about remedying that. Maybe there's a convenient, if tedious in quantity, way to rip from DVD/Blu-Ray? More research needed!

The Master Plan

The plan is to take the old CEI server rack and install it in the downstairs closet. From there, I want it to serve PLEX and backup services to the network, in addition to shared network storage. Obviously, in order to do that, I'm going to need to get it a connection to the router, which is currently located in my office. This can be achieved either through running cable through the walls, or trying to get some bastardized WiFi system setup. Granted, I'm running everything on WiFi at the moment, but I'm less and less satisfied with its performance each day that passes.

That brings up the topic of cabling. Ideally, I would be able to get a hardline drop into every room in the house, including the foyer and living room. Living room has obvious immediate applications, and foyer would be a future proofing type deal. I'll consider the dining room and kitchen too, but that seems like overkill. The office and the bedrooms upstairs are obvious need points, thought.

So that's the really big project. Running cable through walls is a huge hassle to begin with, not to mention the complete lack of mezzanine/crawl space in between floors of my house. That said, I can always look for common walls (the back and right side walls are obvious candidates for everything downstairs except the closet) and just run everything from the attic above the second floor. Then, I can also get drops into all the bedrooms and the office from up there too. This is absolutely a winter/spring project, not a summer project, so keep that in mind.

I figure one or two drops to each room is adequate, with the exception of the closet and maybe the office, due to placement of the coax cable drops for the modem. So figure 4 bedrooms, foyer, living room, puts us at 12 drops. Then the office will need at least 3, so 15 total. I might not be able to fit everything on my switch. I'll have to double check that.

One note here is that while I currently have available a 1 Gbps switch, with (I think) 16 or 24 ports, I don't plan to keep the network at 1 Gbps forever. Ideally, I'd like to be able to drop in a 10 Gbps switch as my devices become capable of using it.

One last note on the subject of network connectivity: I'm thinking that our current Wi-Fi setup isn't powerful enough for the whole house. I get drop outs relatively frequently on my phone while passing Roku audio data from the TV to my bluetooth headphones. I'm thinking that a new setup for WiFi is necessary. It might be as simple as having a secondary (and/or tertiary) access point spread out throughout the house, in addition to no longer relying on my combo modem/router as the only AP.

So after all this networking, we should be setup really well for a high device-count home: 16 ish devices hardwired over 1 Gbps ethernet (eventually 10 Gbps), including the Roku and maybe the TV downstairs (or maybe another Roku if the TV can't handle ethernet), the server and NAS, my PC, and whatever the kids end up with. Then we'll also have multiple APs for good WiFi coverage throughout the house and into the back and front yards as well.

Now onto the construction of the pieces that will make up the endpoints of the network. Currently, we have a 2U, single Xeon server with a couple of hard disks attached, a 1U, 4 drive NAS (with busted old drives that I shouldn't trust to anything). The good news is that the server has USB connectivity (2.0), so I can connect up the big 4 TB backup drive there for regular offline backups. Then I have the aforementioned PCs and media devices. Should come together nicely.

As a side note, let's take a look at what uncompressed video playback bandwidth looks like. 1080p24/32@60 comes out at just shy of 4 Gbps uncompressed. Obviously not going to happen uncompressed over the network. Found some better numbers for compressed playback over the network, and it looks like 4k@30 requires between 25-100 Mbps, so 4k@60 should be no problem (for a single device!). 10 Gbps would obviously be a better expansion option for later on, when more devices come online pushing that type of content.
