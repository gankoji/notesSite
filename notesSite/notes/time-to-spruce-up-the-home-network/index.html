The Scope Creeps

Many moons ago, when I first wrote some basic notes about the kind of network infrastructure I envisioned for the household here, the requirements were simple. We had precisely one desktop and two laptops in the house, along with two mobile phones and two tablets. Most of those things belonged to me, and thus were used rather sparingly.

Fast forward to today, and my son's old laptop has gone to my youngest, since he's now got his very own desktop that he and I built. My oldest daughter has my previous laptop, as she needed it for school. The girls both received my tablets as spur of the moment gifts as well, and my youngest plays with hers nearly every day (her sister uses hers often, just not as much).

Obviously, my wife and I both have our phones as well, and my son is already talking about getting one of his own. Thus, our requirements for network capacity have increased significantly. We need the bandwidth to support this many devices (which we have, in spades). We need the wireless routing capacity to handle them as well (we also have that, for now), and we need a coherent, easy way for dad the tech guy to keep all those devices in tip top shape (and backed up).

As time has gone on, I have more than 1.5TB now of must keep data. Family pictures and movies, along with old academic records, papers, and projects, book collections, various media and video game downloads, and lots more. I have the capacity to hold all that in the desktop, but as time continues to march forward and our data continues to grow, will that still be the case? It's also all in one spot, on one drive that could crash any moment. My only backups are tediously manual ones on external USB hard drives, which I only get to once every 6 months or so. This needs to change!
