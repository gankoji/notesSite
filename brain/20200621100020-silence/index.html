Reading a paper simply titled "Silence", on communication in distributed systems. Since sending messages between agents in a distributed system incurs a cost in storage, bandwidth, energy and CPU time, it is desirable to minimize the system's need for them. This work introduces a framework in which decisions are made via not transmitting messages, where n rounds of complete silence indicate all voting members are in agreement. They then demonstrate the technique for the case of distributed transaction processing in large, online databases.
