Home wifi Sunday, December 29, 2019 9:55 PM

I'm thinking about setting up a mesh network for the wifi at home, to solve the weak signal and dropout issues we have in some spots of the house.

Checked out these articles for product reviews:

  • https://www.ign.com/articles/the-best-mesh-wi-fi-systems-2019
  • https://www.cnet.com/news/best-mesh-wi-fi-system-2019-eero-google-nest-netgear/
  • https://www.pcworld.com/article/3394599/best-mesh-wi-fi-routers.html
  • https://www.pcmag.com/roundup/350795/the-best-wi-fi-mesh-network-systems

The Linksys Velop looks interesting, but its very expensive. Asus and Netgear have options as well, all very expensive ($300+).

Among the research, I stumbled upon notes on the next WiFi standard, WiFi 6, here: https://www.cnet.com/news/wi-fi-6-and-what-it-means-for-you-wifi-routers/. WiFi 6 has a theoretical maximum bandwidth of 10 Gbps, which is pretty fucking fantastic. Ideally, I should setup a WiFi 6 mesh, for futureproofing. None of the current devices will be capable of handling it, but future ones will, and I'll be glad I did.

I strongly dislike the eero idea, since you have to manage them through a mobile app. They require your router to know your identity. Not acceptable.

I do like the ubiquiti amplifi-alien, but it also has an app for management, but it's unclear whether the same privacy concerns abound. Need to dig in.

Looks like it's only necessary for remote access to the router management from outside the Lan. Solid then.

The only remaining issue is price: the amplifi aliens are 379 each rn, and there's no subordinate node types, so it would be 379 for each node, period.

The other option is the linksys velop. 365 for a kit with three nodes, which is a good fit for our house. Need to read reviews.

This TP Link device https://www.pcmag.com/review/362782/tp-link-deco-m9-plus-mesh-wi-fi-system

 Looks promising

So overall the problem looks to be one of price VS capability. The only wifi 6 mesh system I've found is the ubiquiti amplifi, which is going to cost $1k plus for the whole house.

Currently working with a Linksys E2500 (N600 MIMO, old router, about 2009) and a Cox CGM4141COX Modem/Router combo.

Reconfigured the CGM4141 and found that it wasn't using the 5 GHz wireless band at all, and in fact the 2.4 GHz band was 802.11n only, with limited bandwidth. This mostly explains the issues I'd been having with its connectivity, although there were a few hiccups with super low d/l speeds on some devices that weren't necessarily explained by that. They did go away, however, now that we're able to use the 5 GHz band on the same network. I may not need any new equipment at all, at least for the foreseeable future. Still wondering whether wiring the whole house is practical/useful/going to happen. Not at the moment, though.
