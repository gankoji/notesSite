It's still a work in progress. Maybe I'll make some more progress on it this week.

Other odd topics this week: I took a deep dive back into org mode, featuring org-noter and org-ref. Org-noter is a mode that allows you to open an org file and pdf simultaneously, adding notes to the PDF which then reside in the org file, with location links attached to the notes to get you back to the exact spot you were in the PDF when you wrote the note. Also has some nice little bits that follow your notes as you move around in the PDF.

Org-ref is an absolutely fantastic package, and I'm not sure how I ever managed to write LaTeX without it. Essentially, org-ref makes your org files cognizant of your bibtex sources. Additionally, since it includes helm and ivy integration, you can easily zoom through absolutely massive bib files to find just the right source to site, right where you're writing. In addition to trying this out, I also realized that I could have been writing all of my LaTeX in org mode already, since the export is really freaking slick, provided you have all the right CTAN packages installed (which isn't terribly many). I'll never write another LaTeX file manually again, seriously. When you include the ability to hit a keychord, type a phrase, and have a proper citation right inline where you're writing (from org-ref), there's honestly no comparison to how smooth it makes the process. Org-ref also helps you manage cross-referencing figures, tables, and equations, with the same helm completion backend, right inline with your writing. It makes writing LaTeX infinitely more enjoyable.

Final notes are on reference management. I'm still dreading having to use either Zotero or Qiqqa as a reference manager for my dissertation and other research work. Neither has a particularly good interface, although to be fair the real limitation (at least here at work) is my inability to use either app's internet connectedness or browser plugins to auto grab reference information. Org-ref has the same sort of functionality (by drag and dropping a PDF into an org buffer, and/or querying various search engines), which is also limited here at work. That said, google scholar and others have the ability to export bibtex directly, so copy pasting into a bib buffer is almost as easy as using the browser plugin. I do have concerns as to managing the storage of the actual PDF files of my references, which Zotero and Qiqqa are supposed to make easier, but I haven't made a decision on that yet. At the moment, org-ref is winning due to its excellent emacs integration (my tool of choice), and easier user interface than either Zotero's or Qiqqa's manual input modes, which are utter garbage.
